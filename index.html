Cover art for the Journal of Biological Chemistry (JBC) visualizing antibiotic-resistant pathogens:

April 2025 | Volume 301, Issue 4 | Open Access

On the Cover: Depicted are the outer and inner membranes of the common antibiotic resistant pathogen P. aeruginosa. (Out of focus P. aeruginosa (brown) is featured in the background). AlphaFold predictions decorate the two membranes. In green is the outer membrane lipoprotein YaiW. The article discovers that YaiW helps transfer the bactericidal fragment (blue) of the tear, plasma, cerebral spinal fluid and salivary protein lacritin across the outer membrane into the periplasm, the region between both membranes. The article also discovers that the fragment then binds the inner membrane ferrous iron transporter FeoB (red) to inhibit ferrous iron (red spheres) uptake into the cytoplasm. The fragment also binds one subunit of the inner membrane spermidine/putrescine transporter (orange, purple, aqua). This inhibits spermidine and putrescine (black/blue) uptake. The lacritin fragment kills the PA14 pathogenic strain of P. aeruginosa and clinical isolates. P. aeruginosa failed to develop resistance against it.

Published in: Journal of Biological Chemistry

This 3D visualization helps biochemists better understand the structure of antibiotic-resistant bacteria, contributing to scientific communication in the field of biochemical science.
